Quick answer

The Nike Dri-FIT Flex Stride 5 Inch Shorts earn our top spot for their no-nonsense blend of breathability, pocket security, and a chafe-free liner that works for everything from tempo runs to leg day.

If you are after a budget-friendly workhorse, the Under Armour HeatGear Armour Shorts deliver for less; the Lululemon Pace Breaker Shorts are the indulgent premium pick; and the New Balance Impact Run Shorts slot in as a solid mid-range alternative for runners who want a minimalist feel.

Finding the perfect pair of athletic shorts can feel like a quest, you need something that moves with you, wicks sweat, and doesn’t bunch or ride up mid-stride. Whether you’re hammering out miles on the pavement, hitting a HIIT class, or just want a pair that doubles for errands, the right shorts make all the difference. Your choices largely come down to fabric weight, inseam length, and whether you prefer a built-in liner or a brief liner. A shorter inseam (around 5 inches) offers maximum mobility for runners and sprinters, while a 7-inch or 9-inch cut provides more coverage for gym workouts or casual wear. Look for a secure pocket that can hold a phone without bouncing, and pay attention to waistband fit, some have a drawstring, others use a stretchy elastic band with a zip fly. The best shorts disappear on your body, which is exactly what you want.

Best for specific needs

Best for serious running

If your primary activity is pounding pavement, you want a short that won’t hold you back. The Nike Dri-FIT Flex Stride and New Balance Impact Run are both top contenders, but the Nike edges ahead with its secure zip pocket and smoother liner. For pure weight reduction, the New Balance is lighter, but the Nike offers a better balance of features for most runners. Our pick: Nike Men's Dri-FIT Flex Stride 5 Inch Shorts.

Best for hot-weather training

When the mercury rises, moisture management is everything. The Under Armour HeatGear Armour Shorts’ lightweight fabric and compression liner excel at wicking sweat and preventing chafing. They are also the most affordable option, making them an easy choice for summer runs or gym sessions where you’ll be drenched by the end. Our pick: Under Armour Men's HeatGear Armour Shorts.

Best for all-day wear and versatility

Sometimes you need a pair of shorts that transitions seamlessly from a morning workout to grabbing lunch with friends. The Lululemon Pace Breaker Shorts look sharp, feel luxurious, and resist wrinkles and odor. Their 7-inch inseam offers a flattering length that works on and off the gym floor, though the premium price means they are an investment. Our pick: Lululemon Men's Pace Breaker Short 7 Inch.

Our verdict

No single pair of shorts suits every athlete, but the Nike Dri-FIT Flex Stride 5 Inch comes closest. It checks the boxes for breathability, pocket security, and liner comfort at a reasonable price. If your budget is tight, the Under Armour HeatGear Armour is a steal; if you want a premium pair that doubles as casual wear, the Lululemon Pace Breaker is worth the splurge. For dedicated runners, the New Balance Impact Run offers a featherweight alternative, while the adidas Own the Run fills the gap for cooler-weather training with its generous pocket setup. Whichever you choose, look for a short that lets you move freely and forget you’re wearing it, that’s the mark of a real winner.
